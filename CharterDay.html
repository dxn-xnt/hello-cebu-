Cebu Charter Day: A Celebration of History and Joy

About the event

Cebu City Charter Day marks the founding of Cebu City as a chartered city in 1937 through Commonwealth Act No. 58, signed by then-President Manuel L. Quezon. This historic event signifies Cebu City's transformation into the first chartered city in the Philippines outside Metro Manila, paving the way for its rapid growth and development. The occasion is filled with vibrant festivities, including grand civic parades, cultural presentations, and the recognition of outstanding citizens who have contributed to the cityâ€™s progress. Various community-driven activities are held to celebrate Cebu City's rich history, achievements, and cultural heritage, fostering a sense of pride, unity, and belonging among Cebuanos. The event not only honors the city's past but also inspires future generations to continue contributing to its prosperity and progress.

Time and Date:

Cebu City Charter Day is celebrated annually on February 24, with events typically running throughout the day.

Location:

The celebration is held across Cebu City, with key events often taking place at significant locations such as the Cebu City Hall grounds, Plaza Sugbo, and other cultural and civic venues within the city.
