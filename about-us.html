Historical Context

Known as the "Queen City of the South," Cebu holds a unique position in the history of the Philippines. It is recognized as the cradle of Christianity in Asia, being the site where Portuguese explorer Ferdinand Magellan planted the cross in 1521. This marked the introduction of Catholicism to the Philippines. Cebu became the first Spanish settlement and the oldest city in the country, established as a pivotal hub for trade and governance during the Spanish colonial period.

Cebu is a melting pot of diverse cultural influences, blending traditions from its pre-colonial Austronesian roots with Spanish, Chinese, and modern Filipino elements. The Sinulog Festival, celebrated every January in honor of the Santo Niño (Child Jesus), is a testament to Cebu’s vibrant cultural identity. This grand celebration features colorful street parades, traditional dances, and religious processions, attracting visitors from all over the world.

As a municipality, Cebu has grown into an economic powerhouse. It serves as a hub for business, trade, and tourism in the Visayas region, boasting a thriving economy supported by agriculture, fishing, and an ever-growing urban sector. Despite its rapid development, Cebu has maintained its charm, preserving historical landmarks such as Magellan’s Cross, Basilica Minore del Santo Niño, and Fort San Pedro.

Cebu’s scenic landscapes include pristine beaches, lush mountains, and marine sanctuaries, making it a destination for both relaxation and adventure. The municipality also prides itself on being a harmonious blend of traditional community values and modern amenities, fostering a sense of belonging among its residents and visitors..





Cebu city's Officials

The Mayor of Cebu City, Michael Rama, leads the city with a focus on sustainable development and transforming Cebu into a "Singapore-like" city through infrastructure projects and public service improvements. His administration emphasizes urban planning, disaster preparedness, and economic growth.

The Vice Mayor, Raymond Alvin Neri Garcia is the current Vice Mayor of Cebu City. He previously served as Acting Mayor after Michael Rama was suspended in 2024. Known for his practical leadership, Garcia has focused on administrative changes, such as reshuffling department heads and pushing forward key city projects like the Cebu City Medical Center. He emphasizes the importance of civic duty and responsible governance for all city employees, regardless of their political affiliations.

Cebu City's tourism boom can be attributed to the collaborative efforts of local officials who have driven significant initiatives to attract tourists. Mayor Michael Rama and Vice Mayor Raymond Alvin Neri Garcia, along with other leaders, have focused on improving infrastructure, promoting cultural heritage, and enhancing connectivity.

Mayor Rama's leadership has prioritized the development of modern infrastructure and transportation, which has improved both domestic and international tourism access​ Notably, Cebu's airports and ports have been upgraded, facilitating easier travel for tourists. Furthermore, the city's vibrant festivals, such as Sinulog, continue to attract thousands annually, celebrating Cebu's rich cultural heritage​ Vice Mayor Garcia has been instrumental in maintaining environmental sustainability while encouragingtourism growth.

This includes supporting eco-friendly projects and preserving Cebu’s historical landmarks​ Additionally, tourism projects like the regular flights to Bantayan Island and the promotion of the province’s hidden gems have further boosted the influx of visitors​ Together, their collective efforts, focusing on infrastructure, cultural preservation, and accessibility, have made Cebu one of the most visited destinations in the Philippines.
