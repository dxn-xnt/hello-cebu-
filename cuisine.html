Featured Eats: Cebu City's Pride on a Plate

Dive into Cebu City’s rich culinary heritage with a curated selection of its most iconic dishes. From the world-famous lechon to hidden treasures like puso (hanging rice) and seafood delights, these featured eats capture the heart and flavor of Cebuano cuisine. Savor every bite and discover why Cebu is a true foodie paradise!

Cebu Favorites

Siomai sa Tisa

The pride of Tisa! These steamed pork dumplings are a mouthwatering specialty of the district, best enjoyed with a zesty mix of calamansi (limonsito), vinegar, soy sauce, and chile-garlic oil.

Lechon de Cebu

Renowned as the crown jewel of roasted pigs in the Philippines, Cebu lechon boasts irresistibly crispy skin, rich, flavorful meat, and perfection so complete it needs no sauce to steal the show!

Balbacua

A hearty stew of tender beef and gelatinous cuts slow-cooked to silky perfection in a rich, peanut-infused broth. One bite of Balbacua isn’t just a meal—it’s a delicious dive into the vibrant flavors of Cebu’s culinary heritage!

Tuslob Buwa

Cebu's street food gem, is a rich, savory mix of pork liver and brain, sautéed with garlic, onions, shrimp paste, and chili. Simmered in a thick, bubbly stew, it’s perfect for dipping puso (hanging rice) and savoring every flavorful bite!

Ginabot

Golden-battered and deep-fried to crispy perfection, pork intestines are perfectly paired with puso or savored as pulutan. Dip it in tangy vinegar, and you’ve got a flavor-packed Cebuano classic!

Ngohiong

Cebu’s version of spring rolls is a golden delight filled with singkamas, ground pork, and seasoned with aromatic Chinese five-spice. Complemented by a sweet-and-spicy dipping sauce, it’s a flavorful fusion of textures and bold flavors!
